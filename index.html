The Travel Simulator is an open-source airline market simulator built around independent modules. Enjoy it as a whole or use a few modules, it is powerful and flexible.

What is it ?

The Travel Simulator is an open source Airline Market Simulator tool originally developed in C++ by the Amadeus IT Group. You are therefore free to use and modify it according to the GPL3 license terms.

What does it do ?

Simulating an airline market is a bit vague. The objective is to simulate the airline systems (distribution, inventory, schedule, fares, revenue management, etc) and to reproduce individual travellers making decisions regarding itineraries and purchase of airline tickets. After running a simulation, typical KPI (bookings, revenue, load factors, etc), at various aggregation levels, can be retrieved. Consequently, the Travel Simulator enables to replay, in a safe mode, real-world events and prove the efficiency of new Revenue Management methods before bringing them to the real world.

How can I start ?

The Travel Simulator is open source meaning you can download it and modify it freely. For more information you can go to the documentation page where guides and tutorials will help you in your first steps relating to configuration and usage. If you want to jump right in, you can go to the online page and perform your first simulation, or you can get a fully configured virtual machine and import it in your favorite VM manager.
